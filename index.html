  • Course Code: 2010605
  • Number of credits: 03
  • Number of hours Weekly: 04 hours (2T, 2P)

SYLLABUS

SUMILLA

Artificial Intelligence, concepts, paradigms and applications in industry and services. Knowledge representation. Representation of AI problems as search in state space. Blind and informed search methods. Intelligent man-machine games. Expert systems, architecture, taxonomy and applications. Inference Engine. Knowledge engineering, concepts, evolution, CommonKADS Methodology. Quality and validation of Expert Systems, Introduction to Machine learning (Automatic Learning) and heuristics.

SYLLABUS

Objective

Students will acquire knowledge of the Artificial Intelligence area in general and will develop basic aspects in the development of intelligent games and expert systems, and their application in the resolution of intelligent problems in the industrial and service sectors.

CONTENT

WEEK CONTENT RESOURCES LABORATORY WORKS
1
Classification of algorithmic problems
  • Course presentation
  • Classification of algorithmic problems, P and NP.
  • Decision, location and optimization problems.
  • Description of some problems NP- difficult.
2
Fundamentals of artificial intelligence
  • Definition of Artificial Intelligence.
  • Intelligent machine.
  • Difference between operational systems and intelligent systems.
  • Applications in industry and services (robotics, planning, waste management).
  • Turing test.
3
Representation of human - machine game problems as a search in a state space
  • Definition of AI problems as search problems in a state space.
  • Representation of human - machine game problems.
4 y 5
Blind and informed search methods
  • The evaluating function, blind or uninformed search methods: breadth, depth and non-deterministic, methods using additional information: best first, ascent of the hill
6
Search methods for human-machine games
  • Human - machine game algorithm.
  • Machine game strategies: non-deterministic, best first, min-max, and best utility difference.
  • Min-max and alpha-beta algorithm.
7
Presentation of computational works
  • Students will show their skills in developing smart game software based on search techniques.
  • A report and software must be submitted, and their work must be exhibited.
8
Midterm exam
9
Expert Systems Fundamentals
  • Definition of Expert Systems
  • Architecture of an expert system.
  • Taxonomy and applications of expert systems.
  • Requirements for the development of expert systems and advantages of using expert systems
  • Some knowledge-based problems.
10
Knowledge engineering
  • Introduction.
  • Acquisition of knowledge.
  • The CommonKADS methodology.
  • Expert Systems Design (SE).
  • Life cycle of an SE.
11
Knowledge Acquisition
  • Acquisition of knowledge.
  • Building the fact base and knowledge base.
  • Knowledge representation structures (inference rules, frames, objects, semantic networks, predicate logic).
12
Rule-based expert systems development
  • Building the fact base and knowledge base.
  • The inference engine.
  • The regressive chaining methods, progressive and reversibility.
  • Matching techniques, the RETE algorithm.
  • Conflict resolution techniques.
13
Quality and validation of expert systems
  • Major errors in the development of an expert system
  • Quality of an expert system.
  • Smart systems validation, quantitative validation methods.
  • Efficiency and error of expert systems.
  • Review of the functionality of the SE of the 2nd job.
14
Introduction to Machine Learning
  • (Machine Learning) and heuristics.
  • Learning and machine learning concepts.
  • Expert systems vs machine learning.
  • Learning techniques and machine learning development phases.
  • Machine learning applications in industry and services.
  • Concepts of heuristics and meta-heuristics.
  • Exact algorithms vs heuristic algorithms.
  • Heuristic and meta-heuristic techniques.
  • Combinatorial optimization problems in industry and services
15
Presentation of computational works
  • Students will show their skills in developing smart game software based on search techniques.
  • A report and software must be submitted, and their work must be exhibited.
16
Final exam
